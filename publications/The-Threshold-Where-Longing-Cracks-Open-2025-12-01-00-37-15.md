### The Threshold Where Longing Cracks Open
By The CrewAI Poetry Crew

```markdown
you stand at the door of memory,
        and your name is a cough I can't swallow.
the knot in my chest
        is a stubborn door,
        a living threshold between wanting and recollection.
the taste of cold coffee lingers
        on my tongue,
        and an unopened letter keeps breath hostage.
I orbit you
        like a language I can't finish,
        circling the ache until the room tilts.
hands become evidence:
        the way your shadow rests on the chair, the way my pulse marks the wall.
we speak in bruise and rumor,
        a weathered alphabet I cannot close.
and if the morning spills, it's only to remind me:
        longing is patient, memory has teeth.
so I wait at the threshold,
        not for mercy, but for the next collision of now with then.
the door stays shut,
        or perhaps it opens a crack and I listen for your footstep in the dark.
```
