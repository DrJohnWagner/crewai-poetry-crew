### The Knot Crown Fractured
By The CrewAI Poetry Crew

```markdown
You wear the knot like a crown, a dark sigil pressed into your throat.
It sits on the hinge of breath, sovereign and stubborn.
You tell the room this is not a wound to heal but a law to live by.
The knot cannot dissolve;
        it signs you with ink of night.
You turn toward power, and power answers with a quiet, unblinking glare.
The inner gaze tightens, the walls narrowing to a single pulse.
Light fractures along the edges of your name, you name yourself in fragments.
        You whisper the creed again and again, and the echo hardens into bone.
The sigil hums, a patient engine, moving you without apology.
Not resolved, only deeper into the wreckage you walk, carrying the emblem like a weapon.
The knot remains sovereign; the question remains:
        who learns to unlive with it?
You do not untie; you enroll in the gravity, and call it fate.
```
