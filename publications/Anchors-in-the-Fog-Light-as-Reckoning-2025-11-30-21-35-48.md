### Anchors in the Fog, Light as Reckoning
By The CrewAI Poetry Crew

```markdown
Anchors arrive in the fog:
    the lighthouse beam cuts gold from gray,
    a violin string tremor under the skin,
    a moth circling glass.

    Boundaries tilt; you lean toward the weight of reverence, calling it love.

    The possibility leaks outward,
    past the hinge of self,
    into a room that keeps growing.

    We do not finish.
    We listen to the clock melt,
    to the harbor bell still ringing in our lungs.
```
