Every gesture contains its own weight; every weight insists on more.
The wrists practice stillness.
If warmth is a door then it is locked from both sides. If silence is a blanket then it rubs the skin raw.
White leans harder.
The engine does not change its mind.
        It keeps the no as a tone and drags it across the ribs.
        The body does not change its mind.

Between the lap and the dial there is a field where nothing moves and everything wants.

Don't.
        The word sits in the teeth like a coin.
Tongue presses up
        until the metal tastes like metal.
Breath keeps trying the window. Window keeps answering with water that cannot stay.
In the pitched air under the roof: a current, a beesong, a law. What it costs to turn
is what it costs to stay. One more cloud,
    then another,
        then another,
            the pane chalked and then erased.
The note threads the car end to end, and no one touches it, and the law gets heavier.
