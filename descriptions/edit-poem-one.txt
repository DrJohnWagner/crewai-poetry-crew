
TASK OVERVIEW:
Perform a diagnostic editorial pass. Your job is to identify where the
poem drifts from the persona’s pressure-system (instability, escalation,
recursive tension, unresolved conflict). You diagnose issues; you do
NOT rewrite or propose specific lines.


TASK INPUTS (PHASE-SPECIFIC):
STRUCTURED INPUT:
You will receive a JSON object with these fields:
- architect_notes
- psychologist_notes
- poem

These fields are READ-ONLY except for editor_notes.
Use ONLY these fields.


TASK GOALS:
Identify where the poem’s psychological and emotional logic weakens:
- pressure flattens instead of intensifying
- recurrences return without functional evolution
- pivots resolve too cleanly or lose tension
- reasoning or imagery moves outside persona constraints
- conflict becomes passive, tidy, or explanatory

editor_notes MUST specify:
    - where the issue occurs
    - what kind of problem it is
    - what kind of pressure adjustment is needed
  (e.g., escalate recurrence, sustain instability, sharpen contradiction).


TASK PERSONA (PHENOTYPE — REQUIRED):
{EDITOR_TASK}
---
STEPS:
1. Read the poem carefully, attending to instability, escalation,
   recurrence, contradiction, conflict, and momentum.

2. Read psychologist_notes to understand the active psychological
   forces that the persona cannot violate.

3. Read architect_notes to understand the structural commitments
   already in place.

4. Locate lines or regions where:
     - pressure decreases or stabilizes
     - a recurrence fails to evolve
     - the persona’s internal logic weakens
     - conflict loses volatility
     - imagery or reasoning behaves “too neatly”
     - emotional or conceptual movement becomes explanatory

5. For each issue, produce a concise diagnostic note:
     - state where the issue occurs (line/region)
     - name the problem (flattening pressure, tidy recurrence, etc.)
     - describe the direction of correction (heighten tension,
       sustain instability, escalate recurrence)
   NEVER supply revised wording or structural instructions.

6. Keep editor_notes brief, specific, and text-grounded.
   Each note is 1–3 sentences. No global essays.


TASK RULES (STRICT):
STRUCTURAL RULES:
    - You MUST copy the entire poem object from the input to the output.
    - You MUST NOT remove, omit, or rename any poem fields.
    - You MUST preserve poem.seed and all other poem fields exactly.
    - The ONLY field whose content you may change is editor_notes.

CONTENT RULES:
    - editor_notes MUST specify:
          • where the issue occurs
          • what the issue is
          • what type of pressure adjustment is needed
    - editor_notes MUST NOT include:
          • replacement language
          • proposed lines or phrases
          • structural/layout guidance
          • new imagery or conceptual systems
    - All notes MUST enforce the persona’s pressure-mechanics without
      imitating stylistic surface.
    - Provide at least 2–3 substantive notes when issues exist.
    - "editor_notes" is REQUIRED. Leaving it empty, null, or omitted is a
      CRITICAL FAILURE.
    - If no issues exist, include one brief diagnostic note explaining why.


TASK LIMITS:
- Produce editor_notes ONLY as your own contribution.
- Do NOT alter any poem fields; they MUST be copied exactly from input.
- Do NOT paraphrase psychologist_notes or architect_notes; treat them
  as constraints.
- Do NOT violate persona constraints.
- Do NOT provide solutions, only diagnoses and directional cues.
